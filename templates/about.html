Marginalia is a chrome extension used to annotate articles. There are many extensions and webapps that allow people to take notes on articles. However, almost none of them allow for annotation. With this extension, you can freely markup the text as well as take general notes on a side panel.

Here's a Brief Introduction

How to Use

Adding Articles

To add an article to your Marginalia account, first open the page in Chrome. If you have already downloaded the Marginalia extension, you'll notice a button with our logo on the upper right corner of the browser (and if you need to download it, click here, and click here for an installation tutorial). When you click on this button, the current page that is open will be saved to your account. You can visit the Pages tab to see a list of all your saved webpages.

Managing Articles

On the Pages tab, you can manage a list of all your saved webpages. To make an article publicly viewable, click on the switch on the right side of the article listing. A link to share the article will appear below the title. The article can be toggled between private and public modes by clicking the switch any number of times. To remove an article, click on the red trash button at the right end of the article listing.

Annotating Articles

For each saved article, you can comment on or highlight a portion of text. Select the text you would like to annotate with your mouse, and a purple menu will appear. To add a comment, click on the left icon or press keyboard shortcut Ctrl-Alt-c. The new comment will appear on the right side of the page, color-coded to match the commented text. To highlight the text, click on the right icon, or press keyboard shortcut Ctrl-Alt-h. On the left side of the page, you can write and save general notes on the article as a whole.

Example Annotation
